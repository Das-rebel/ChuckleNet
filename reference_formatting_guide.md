## 📋 **CITATION FORMAT REQUIREMENTS**

### **ACL/EMNLP 2026 Citation Style**
**Format**: ACL Author's Guide (https://acl2026.org/author-guide)
**Style**: APA-like with computational linguistics conventions

#### **In-Text Citations**
- Single Author: (Smith, 2020) or Smith (2020)
- Two Authors: (Smith & Jones, 2021) or Smith and Jones (2021)
- Three+ Authors: (Smith et al., 2022) or Smith et al. (2022)
- Multiple Sources: (Smith, 2020; Jones, 2021)

#### **Reference List Format**
**Journal Articles**:
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. *Journal Name*, *Volume*(Issue), pages. DOI

**Conference Papers**:
Author, A. A., Author, B. B., & Author, C. C. (Year). Title of paper. In *Proceedings of Conference Name* (pp. xxx-xxx).

### **COLING 2026 Citation Style**
**Format**: COLING Author Guidelines (https://coling2026.org/authors)
**Style**: Similar to ACL with international emphasis

#### **Reference List Format**
**Conference Proceedings**:
Author, A. A., Author, B. B. (Year). Title of paper. In *Proceedings of COLING 2026* (pp. xxx-xxx). International Conference on Computational Linguistics.

- [ ] **All in-text citations have corresponding references**

- [ ] **ACL/EMNLP format compliance** (hanging indent, italics, punctuation)

- [ ] **Consistent capitalization** (sentence case for titles, journal names)
- [ ] **Proper abbreviations** (vol., no., pp., DOI format)

### **Quality Check**
- [ ] **Peer-reviewed sources preferred** (avoid predatory journals)

- [ ] **Seminal works cited** (foundational biosemotic and NLP papers)
